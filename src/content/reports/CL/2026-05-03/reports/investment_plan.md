Valuation concerns are overblown: CL trades below analyst consensus price targets despite analyst fair value estimates well above ~$85. The dividend increase and consistent 128-year payment history reinforce investor confidence and provide downside support. Margin compression from input costs is real but mitigated by pricing power and the SG&PP efficiencies. While leverage worries are notable, they're manageable given CL's established cash flows. The institutional selling narrative needs context: despite Danica Pension's exit, major holders like Vanguard and Wealthfront increased positions. The bear's technical assessment is accurate—momentum is choppy—but that argues for a measured building of exposure rather than avoiding the trade. I'm overweight because CL offers growth potential in international markets, a defensive profile for volatile environments, and reasonable valuation that justifies gradual position growth once technical conditions normalize.
