**Rationale**: This debate centers on whether Costco's premium valuation and macro risks outweigh its impressive growth momentum and competitive moats. Costco's membership model provides real defensibility with 200M global members and high stickiness. Despite the bull's high P/E (52.64x), it's justified by strong fundamentals and growth potential - investors are paying for outperformance versus peers, and Costco delivers. The technical picture supports further upside with the stock above key SMAs (50-day at 993.45, 200-day at 947.74), moving higher, and positive analyst consensus (85% Buy/Strong Buy). The bear's macro concerns about Fed policy and rate cuts are valid but secondary to Costco's defensive positioning with low Beta (0.978). When inflation is high, Costco's bulk-buy model and fuel discounts shine, while in calmer markets, its stability makes it attractive. While there are legitimate concerns about valuation and potential technical pullbacks, the combined evidence of strong growth, competitive advantages, institutional support (net buying pressure), and macro tailwinds makes Costco worth increasing exposure to. The bull's arguments carry more weight: the membership model is defensible, institutional demand is strong, and the current price is still below some analyst targets like $1,225. The bear's concerns would be more compelling if costco were underperforming peers - instead, it's outperforming WMT and BJ's.
